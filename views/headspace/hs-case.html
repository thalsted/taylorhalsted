Headspace Feature Proposal
how to improve user retention with crowdsourced sentiment data

User Research

In order to design the right solution, you need to know what problem you're solving... and whose problem it is. For this project, I built a survey to understand users' motivations, aspirations, challenges and pain points. Using a Facebook ad campaign, I targeted people interested in meditation to boost feedback, then loaded survey data for analysis.

User Analysis

Analysis showed that users who meditate more regularly find it easier to maintain the habit. Isolating reasons that users struggle would help to inform features that encourage a healthy routine. By distilling responses into composite dimensions, I found that respondents fell into one of three groups. Understanding the characteristics of each group allowed me to build personas that would become the foundation for driving higher retention.

Problem & Opportunity

For each persona there was a story — from each story, a unique problem to solve. Prioritizing based on potential opprtunity, I chose to focus on the group that represented almost half of users. Survey responses showed that this subset wanted a personalized feel; they had an appetite for free, varied content and a sense of community.

Market Validation

An Indiana University study showed that the attrition rate for people working toward a physical fitness goal in pairs was dramatically lower (85% lower, to be exact) than those working alone. It turned out users knew this intuitively, and were trying to synthesize a community on their own. Users' personal solutions often lead to the requirements for a better product.

Product Requirements

Legendary MMA trainer Frank Shamrock stressed the importance of surrounding ourselves with the right people. His methodology suggests we need a mentor, an equal, and a protégé if we want to be successful. Following this approach, I propose a feature to circulate motivation & wisdom across the Headspace journey. By allowing users to learn and teach, they can stay accountable to themselves and their community.
